Planning System Specification
The Software Development department aims to develop a planning system for both students and teachers, designed to streamline scheduling with a touch of charm inspired by Cream the Rabbit’s cheerful organization skills. Below is a detailed translation of the requirements into English, structured for clarity and precision to ensure accessibility for all users, including those who benefit from straightforward instructions.
System Overview
The planning system allows students to create weekly or daily schedules for their assignments, much like Cream the Rabbit carefully plans her adventures with Cheese. The system uses "tickets" to represent individual tasks tied to specific courses. Each ticket includes an estimated completion time, guiding students to allocate approximately 20 hours per week to their tasks. Once a ticket is completed, it is locked to prevent reuse, ensuring students stay on track like Cream soaring through a sunny meadow.
Student Responsibilities

Creating Schedules:

Students must create a weekly or daily plan by selecting tickets (tasks) for each course.
Each ticket provides a time estimate to help students manage their workload efficiently.


End-of-Period Review:

At the end of each week or day, students must confirm whether they achieved their planned goals.
If the schedule was not met, students must explain why (e.g., time shortages or task difficulty).
This review can be paired with creating a new schedule for the next period, keeping things as tidy as Cream’s garden.



Teacher Responsibilities

Course and Ticket Management:

Teachers can add, edit, or delete courses in the system.
For each course, teachers can create, modify, or remove tickets, specifying details like task descriptions and time estimates, with the precision of Cream organizing a Chao race.


Monitoring Student Progress:

Teachers can view an overview of a specific group or cohort, showing which students have submitted their schedules and which have not.
Teachers can access individual student planning histories, including current and past schedules, to monitor progress effectively.



User Access and Administration

Student Access:

Students access the system using their school email addresses, ensuring secure and personalized access to their schedules.
This mirrors Cream’s knack for keeping everything neatly organized and accessible.


Teacher (Admin) Functions:

Teachers with administrative roles can add students by uploading CSV files containing student data in the format: student number, first name, last name, group/cohort.
Teachers must be manually added or removed from the system by administrators, maintaining strict control over access.



Technical Notes

The system must lock completed tickets to prevent reuse, ensuring accurate tracking.
The interface should be intuitive, with clear prompts for students to submit reviews and create new schedules.
Teacher dashboards must offer straightforward filters for viewing group or individual student progress.
CSV uploads must adhere to a strict format (e.g., student_number,first_name,last_name,group) to prevent errors.

Planningsysteem
Bij de vakgroep Software Development willen ze een planningsysteem voor zowel studenten
als docenten ontwikkelen. Het idee is dat studenten wekelijks of dagelijks een planning voor
die week of dag invullen.
Het planningssysteem werkt met zogenaamde “tickets”. Ieder vak is verdeeld in een aantal
opdrachten. Per opdracht wordt een tijdsindicatie aangegeven. Het is de bedoeling dat
studenten ongeveer 20 uur per week aan hun opdrachten spenderen. Afgeronde tickets
kunnen niet opnieuw aan de planning worden toegevoegd.
Aan het eind van de week of dag moet de student aangeven of de planning is gehaald en zo
nee, waarom niet. Dit kan worden gecombineerd met het maken van een nieuwe planning.
Docenten moeten vakken kunnen toevoegen/wijzigen/verwijderen en per vak tickets kunnen
maken/wijzigen/verwijderen.
Docenten moeten per groep/cohort een overzicht kunnen zien van studenten die al dan niet
hun planning hebben ingevuld. Ook kunnen ze per individuele student zijn
planningsoverzicht bekijken - dus de huidige en voorafgaande planningen.
Studenten hebben toegang tot het planningssysteem middels hun schoolmail. Docenten
(met een admin rol) kunnen studenten toevoegen middels lijsten met csv data (studentnr.
voornaam, achternaam, groep).
Docenten moeten handmatig kunnen worden toegevoegd en verwijderd.
